## Issues Found & Fixed

### Major Issues (3)

**Issue 1**: [Plot Logic] Information leak mechanism unclear in Chapter 8
- **Location**: /home/user/CodexClaude3/Outlines/Standalone/Outline Chapter 08.md, Scene 4
- **Problem**: How Jonathan Rhys discovered the Cornwall location was insufficiently explained. Chapter 1 established Astrid told her supervisor "not the location," but Chapter 8 originally suggested she told Eleanor "private UK collection." The mechanism of how Rhys connected Marlowe & Sons → UK travel → specific Ashford estate needed clarification.
- **Fix**: Rewrote the explanation to show Astrid logged UK travel dates in firm calendar system (without location details), and Rhys pieced together: UK trip + Cardano manuscript timing + research into recent Cardano estate acquisitions + genealogical records + contacted sources = discovered Ashford connection. This makes the discovery plausible through competitive intelligence gathering rather than direct information leak.
- **Impact**: Plot logic now solid. Explains how antagonist found them without requiring Astrid to have been careless with information, which would be out of character.

**Issue 2**: [Plot Logic] USB drive evidence subplot unresolved in Chapters 14-16
- **Location**: /home/user/CodexClaude3/Outlines/Standalone/Outline Chapter 15.md, Scene 2
- **Problem**: James Cavendish gives protagonists explosive evidence (USB drive with internal Ashworth Holdings communications) in Chapter 14, but this evidence disappeared from the narrative. Chapter 15 mentioned they can't use it directly (stolen proprietary information), but there was no clear resolution of how it factored into their defense strategy or what happened to it.
- **Fix**: Added explicit conversation with lawyer Rachel Torres in Chapter 15 Scene 2 where she confirms they can't introduce the USB as evidence but can use the information strategically to anticipate opponents' arguments and preemptively counter with independently obtained evidence. This closes the loop and shows the evidence served a purpose (roadmap for defense) without creating legal liability.
- **Impact**: Subplot now has clear resolution. The Cavendish evidence has narrative purpose and logical conclusion, strengthening the legal defense arc.

**Issue 3**: [Character Consistency] Bedroom arrangement surprise inconsistent with Astrid's character in Chapter 4
- **Location**: /home/user/CodexClaude3/Outlines/Standalone/Outline Chapter 04.md, Chapter End
- **Problem**: Original text showed Astrid and Benedict exchanging "a look of dawning realization" that they'd be sharing a room, with "Neither had thought to specify separate rooms." This is inconsistent with Astrid's established meticulous, detail-oriented character who plans everything carefully and makes compulsive lists.
- **Fix**: Revised to show Astrid HAD anticipated this when Lord Ashford's secretary mentioned "committed couples" requirement, but deliberately avoided discussing it during planning because it felt "too intimate, too real." Now facing the practical reality of the shared bedroom. This maintains her forward-thinking character while explaining the apparent oversight through emotional avoidance rather than lack of planning.
- **Impact**: Character consistency maintained. Astrid's behavior now aligns with her established traits (meticulous planner) while adding emotional depth (avoiding intimate topics out of discomfort).

### Minor Issues (2)

**Issue 4**: [Timeline Continuity] Imprecise timeline references between Chapters 1-22
- **Location**: /home/user/CodexClaude3/Outlines/Standalone/Outline Chapter 22.md, Scene 4
- **Problem**: Chapter 22 originally stated "Six months after first meeting Lord Ashford in Cornwall, eight months after the coffee shop." Mathematical calculation of intervening events (Cornwall trip ~2 weeks, crisis period ~2 months, dating period ~2-3 months, moving/settling ~1 month) suggested total timeline of 6-7 months from coffee shop, not 8 months. The specific numbers created an inconsistency.
- **Fix**: Changed to more flexible phrasing: "Over half a year since first meeting Lord Ashford in Cornwall, nearly a year since the coffee shop." This maintains the sense of time passage without creating mathematical contradictions, and "nearly a year" is more accurate for 6-7 months than "eight months" was.
- **Impact**: Timeline now internally consistent. Flexible language allows for realistic time passage without contradicting specific event sequences.

**Issue 5**: [Geography] Travel route reference unclear in Chapter 11
- **Location**: /home/user/CodexClaude3/Outlines/Standalone/Outline Chapter 11.md, Transition from Previous
- **Problem**: Chapter 11 transition said "Back in New York after two weeks in Cornwall" which was geographically incomplete. Actual route was: New York → London → Cornwall → London → New York. The reference made it sound like they returned directly from Cornwall, omitting the London connection.
- **Fix**: Revised to: "Back in New York after two weeks away—the flight from London to JFK reversing the journey that started this all." This accurately reflects the return route (London to NYC) while maintaining narrative flow.
- **Impact**: Geographic accuracy improved. Route now matches established travel pattern and provides clearer sense of the journey.
