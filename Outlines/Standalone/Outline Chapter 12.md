# Chapter 12: Pressure Points

**Transition from Previous**: Days after returning to New York. Astrid and Benedict continue working on their publications while facing increasing pressure to suppress or discredit their findings.

**POV**: Astrid Whitlock | **Purpose**: Escalate external threats, deepen relationship under pressure, introduce specific antagonist, test their commitment to truth

**Scene 1 - Anonymous Contact**: Astrid is at her apartment working on the final authentication report when she receives an email from an anonymous address. Subject line: "Regarding the Cardano Manuscript." The message is brief and chilling: "Miss Whitlock, your authentication of the so-called Cardano manuscript is based on insufficient evidence and questionable methodology. I strongly suggest you withdraw your report and recommend the manuscript be re-evaluated by independent experts. Failure to do so will result in professional consequences. Your relationship with Dr. Shaw has compromised your objectivity. Consider this a courtesy warning." No signature, no identifying information, just a veiled threat wrapped in professional language. Astrid's hands shake as she forwards it to Benedict with the message: "Got this today. You?" His reply is immediate: "Similar. Mine mentioned 'reputation damage' and suggested my decryption was 'wishful thinking, not science.' Someone's coordinating this." They meet at a coffee shop in Midtown, both rattled. Benedict shows her his email—from a different anonymous address but similar tone, questioning his methodology and suggesting Clare's recent "concerns" indicate his work is unreliable. "Clare didn't raise concerns," Benedict says tightly. "Someone's using her name to undermine me." Astrid feels cold. This is more than academic rivalry or auction house competition. This is targeted intimidation. "What do we do?" "We find out who's behind it. And we don't back down." Benedict's jaw is set, determined, and Astrid feels a surge of affection for his stubbornness.

**Scene 2 - Investigation**: They spend the afternoon digging into who might want the manuscript's contents suppressed. Benedict focuses on the historical angle—families descended from people named in the cipher. Astrid researches recent auction activity, looking for patterns in who's been buying up Renaissance materials, especially anything related to the Italian Wars or papal history. By evening, they have several possibilities. A British noble family descended from one of the conspirators in the Elizabeth assassination plot. An Italian foundation that owns several Medici properties and controls historical narratives about the family. A Vatican-affiliated organization that acquires sensitive religious materials. "Any of them could have the resources and motivation," Astrid says, frustrated. "We need to narrow it down." Benedict has been running network analysis on the email headers, trying to trace their origin despite the anonymization. "They're routing through multiple servers, but there's a pattern that suggests London as the origin point. And look—" He shows her traffic data. "Multiple emails sent to different addresses on the same schedule. They're not just targeting us; they're mounting a campaign." Astrid thinks about Willoughby, his smooth threats, his mysterious clients. "Kensington Rare Books is based in London. What if Willoughby is the middle man?" "Then we need to figure out who hired him." They're sitting at Astrid's dining table surrounded by research, and she realizes they're doing what they do best—solving a puzzle together, combining her historical expertise with his analytical skills. This is why they work, why this relationship feels right despite its problematic beginning.

**Scene 3 - Support and Vulnerability**: Late that night, still working, Astrid's exhaustion breaks into frustration. "Maybe we should just publish and deal with the fallout. Stop trying to manage all these variables." Benedict looks up from his laptop. "Are you having second thoughts about publication?" "No. Yes. I don't know." She pushes away from the table, paces. "I spent my whole career building a reputation for integrity and accuracy. Now I'm facing accusations of bias and compromised judgment, and the worst part is they're partially right. I did compromise my judgment—I lied to Lord Ashford to get access to this manuscript." "Astrid—" "What if everything that follows is tainted by that original lie? What if I don't deserve this success because of how I obtained it?" Benedict stands, stops her pacing, makes her look at him. "Listen to me. You made a questionable choice for good reasons. You've since faced consequences—confessed to Lord Ashford, dealt with professional judgment, carried the guilt. You don't get to use that one mistake to undermine everything you've accomplished. The authentication is sound. The manuscript is real. Your work is excellent. Own that." His fierce certainty steadies her. "How are you so sure?" "Because I know you. I've watched you work, seen your standards, witnessed your integrity even when it's difficult. One lie doesn't erase all of that." Astrid feels tears prick her eyes. "I'm scared. Of the threats, of losing my career, of dragging you down with me—" "Hey." He cups her face gently. "We're in this together. Whatever happens, we face it together. That's what this is, right? Partnership." She nods, unable to speak, and kisses him—grateful and desperate and honest. They hold each other in her quiet apartment while the city hums outside, finding strength in connection.

**Scene 4 - The Call**: Around midnight, Astrid's phone rings. Unknown number, London area code. She answers on speaker so Benedict can hear. "Miss Whitlock? This is Helena Marsh. We met briefly at Ashford Hall." Dr. Marsh, the authenticator who came with Willoughby. Astrid's guard goes up immediately. "What can I do for you, Dr. Marsh?" "I'm calling as a professional courtesy. The people I work for are prepared to make you an offer. £50,000 to withdraw your authentication and recommend the manuscript for further study. Dr. Shaw would receive the same for ceasing his decryption analysis. You could claim you need more time, that preliminary findings were premature. No one needs to know the real reason." "That's a bribe," Astrid says flatly. "That's compensation for reconsidering a rushed judgment. The manuscript is controversial, Miss Whitlock. Publication will damage reputations, upset powerful families, cause international incidents. Is that really what you want?" "I want historical truth documented and preserved. That's my job." "Your job is whatever your employer pays you for. Marlowe & Sons would benefit more from £50,000 than from a donation that earns them nothing." Dr. Marsh's voice is cool, reasonable. "Think about it. Discuss with Dr. Shaw. You have twenty-four hours to decide. After that, other measures will be necessary." The call disconnects. Astrid and Benedict stare at each other in stunned silence. "Other measures," Benedict repeats quietly. "That sounds like a threat." "It was definitely a threat." Astrid's mind races. "£100,000 total to suppress our work. That's serious money, which means serious motivation." "And if we refuse?" "Then they escalate. Discrediting, maybe. Or worse." Benedict runs a hand through his hair, thinking. "We need to go public immediately. Get ahead of this. Publish the authentication and the decryption before they can suppress it." "If we publish now, we make enemies of very powerful people." "We already have. At least this way the truth is out there." Astrid knows he's right, but the fear is real—for her career, for his, for their safety. "Twenty-four hours. Let's use it to finalize everything, make sure the publication is airtight. Then we release it all and damn the consequences." Benedict nods, grim determination on his face. "Together." "Together," Astrid echoes, and they turn back to their work, racing against a deadline that's suddenly become dangerous.

---

**Chapter Arc**: Intimidation begins → investigation of threats → vulnerability and mutual support → direct bribe offer and threat escalation. Act 2 rising action: external pressure testing their commitment to truth and each other.

**Chapter End**: As dawn breaks over New York, Astrid and Benedict are still working, finalizing their publications. They've made their choice—publish the truth regardless of threats. The authentication report is complete, the cryptographic analysis is ready, and all they need is to submit to their respective outlets. "Last chance to back out," Astrid says, only half-joking. "Not a chance," Benedict replies. "You?" "I'm terrified. But I'm more afraid of what I'd become if I let threats silence the truth." They submit their work simultaneously—Astrid to the Journal of Renaissance Studies, Benedict to Cryptologia and the academic pre-print server. It's done. The truth is out there, and now they wait for the consequences.

**Transition to Next Chapter**: Immediate aftermath of publication, public response, escalation of threats to something more dangerous.
