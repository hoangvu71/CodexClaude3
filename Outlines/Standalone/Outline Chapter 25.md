# Chapter 25: Reflection

**Transition from Previous**: Final month before the wedding. Astrid and Benedict take time to reflect on their journey while making last preparations.

**POV**: Benedict Shaw | **Purpose**: Reflect on growth and change, callback to earlier story elements, build emotional depth before wedding, show character evolution

**Scene 1 - Bachelor Party**: Benedict's bachelor party is characteristically low-key—dinner and climbing with a few close friends from Oxford and some London acquaintances. They end up at a pub, and conversation turns to marriage, relationships, how Benedict and Astrid met. "I heard it involved a Renaissance manuscript and fake identities," one colleague says, clearly fishing for the story. Benedict laughs. "Something like that. We met professionally, worked together on an authentication project, fell for each other in the process. The details are complicated." "Most good love stories are," another friend says philosophically, already a few pints in. "Complicated beginnings make for interesting marriages." Later, walking home through late summer London, Benedict thinks about their complicated beginning. The lies, the deception, the ethical compromises. He doesn't regret any of it—couldn't regret the path that led him here—but he's grateful they've moved beyond it to something honest and real. At the flat, Astrid is at her own gathering with Rachel, Emma, and some British Library colleagues. He texts her: "How's your night?" Her reply: "Good. Missing you though. When are you home?" "Ten minutes." "I'll be here." Such a simple exchange, but it fills Benedict with contentment. This is what he wanted—someone to come home to, someone who misses him during a few hours apart, someone who'll be there.

**Scene 2 - Looking Back**: The next day, Benedict goes through old files on his computer, organizing before the wedding, and finds the emails from when Astrid first contacted him about the Cardano project. Her professional tone, the careful explanation of what she needed, the subtext of desperation he'd heard even then. He finds his analysis notes from Cornwall, pages of cipher work interleaved with observations about Astrid—her precision, her passion, the way she pushed her hair back when concentrating. He'd been documenting his own falling in love without quite admitting it. He finds photos from their time at Ashford Hall that they took to maintain the fake engagement fiction: the two of them in the gardens, at dinner, the staged casualness that doesn't look staged at all because somewhere in the performance they'd become real. "What are you looking at?" Astrid asks, coming into his office space. He shows her the photos, the emails. "Evidence of our origin story." She sits beside him, scrolling through. "We look happy even then. I was terrified, but you can't see it in the pictures." "You hid it well." "Professional appraiser. I'm good at controlled presentations." She's quiet for a moment. "I was so focused on the manuscript, on my career, on maintaining the deception. I didn't let myself see what was actually happening between us until I couldn't ignore it anymore." "When did you know?" Benedict asks. "That it was real?" "The night in the hotel after we left Ashford Hall. When you said we'd figure it out together, no matter what came. That's when I knew I'd fallen for you and couldn't unfeel it." She looks at him. "You?" "Earlier. Probably when you suggested the cipher key might be personal to Cardano. You saw the problem differently than I did, and your insight was perfect. That's when I realized I didn't just respect your expertise—I was gone for you entirely." They sit together, looking at evidence of their history, and Benedict feels grateful for every step that brought them here.

**Scene 3 - Benedict's Book**: Benedict's book on Renaissance cryptography is complete and submitted to his publisher. The manuscript (appropriately enough) documents his journey through Cardano's cipher and expands into broader analysis of period encryption methods. In the acknowledgments, he writes: "To Astrid, who taught me that some truths can't be decoded logically but must be lived and trusted. Your partnership made this work possible and my life meaningful." Astrid cries when she reads it, the good kind of tears. "You didn't have to—" "I wanted to. You're part of this work, part of everything I do now." His department head at Oxford calls to congratulate him on completion and mention that the fellowship might be extended or converted to a permanent position if he's interested. It's the kind of opportunity that would have consumed him entirely a year ago. Now, Benedict thinks about what it would mean: more time in Oxford, less in London, less time with Astrid. "I'll think about it," he tells his supervisor, but he already knows the answer. Professional success matters, but not more than partnership, not more than the life he's built with Astrid. He'll find a way to balance—perhaps more remote work, perhaps seeking opportunities closer to London. They'll figure it out together.

**Scene 4 - Pre-Wedding Jitters**: A week before the wedding, Benedict has a moment of panic. Not about Astrid, not about marriage itself, but about deserving this happiness. His first marriage failed. He was emotionally absent, chose work over connection, let something that could have worked dissolve through neglect. What if he repeats those patterns? What if he fails Astrid the way he failed Clare? He calls Emma, his usual sounding board. "I'm terrified I'll mess this up. That I'll revert to my worst habits—getting lost in work, forgetting she exists, taking her for granted." "Are you doing those things now?" Emma asks practically. "No. But what if—" "Benedict. Stop catastrophizing. You're different with Astrid because she's different for you. Clare needed someone you couldn't be; Astrid needs someone you already are. Trust that." "What if it's not enough?" "What if it is? What if you're enough exactly as you are, and she loves you knowing all your flaws and habits and worst tendencies? What if you get to be happy without earning it through perfection?" The idea settles something in Benedict. He doesn't have to be perfect. He just has to be present, honest, trying. That he can do. That evening, he tells Astrid about his fears. She listens, then takes his hands. "I'm scared too. That I'll be too rigid, too controlling, that I'll try to perfect something that should be organic. But we can be scared together and choose each other anyway. That's what marriage is—choosing someone despite fear, because love matters more." "I choose you," Benedict says. "Always have, even when I was pretending I didn't." "I choose you too. Every day, intentionally, for the rest of my life." The fear doesn't disappear completely, but it recedes to manageable. They'll be okay. They'll work at it, communicate, choose each other daily like Benedict's father advised. That's enough.

---

**Chapter Arc**: Reflection on journey → reviewing history → professional completion → confronting pre-wedding fears. Emotional preparation for marriage, acknowledging growth and residual fears while reaffirming commitment.

**Chapter End**: Late that night, Benedict can't sleep. He gets up, walks through their home—books, Benedict's climbing gear, Astrid's authentication equipment, Gio asleep in a patch of streetlight. Evidence of their life together. In his office, he opens the old notebook where they wrote their fake cover story. The fabricated first kiss, the invented weekend in Boston, the performance of falling in love. He adds an entry in the margin: "All of this became true. Every lie we told transformed into reality. Proof that sometimes fiction is just prophecy waiting to happen." He closes the notebook, puts it back in its box. In one week they'll be married. The fake engagement will become real, legal, permanent. And Benedict, who spent years believing he wasn't built for lasting love, is ready to promise forever.
